A Mobile Application for ADHD Analysis & Detection

Project Scope


Literature Survey

Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder is a common mental disorder, which can be identified mostly among children which often lasts till their adulthood. A person with ADHD would not be able to pay attention, not think about consequences of their actions(impulsivity) and sometimes could be hyperactive (overly active). According to the ADHD Institute statistical reports worldwide prevalence of ADHD, of ~2.2% overall (range, 0.1-8.1%) has been estimated only within the children and adolescents (aged < 18 years)

Research Gap

Most of the existing applications for ADHD focuses on screening and treatments and do not consider the diagnosing


Research Problem

Even though there are many clinical ways to identify ADHD, it is not an easy task to diagnose symptoms. Children these days are overactive and distracted by the smallest thing and it is perfectly normal, but some kids suffer from ADHD and even they might not know it. There are screening tools that diagnose ADHD, but they are focused on adults, not children


Research Objectives

Implement an effective and dynamic mobile application to detect symptoms of ADHD using the DSM-5 criteria and guide the person to the treatments


Methodology

Application is an android native with activities such as questionnaires (text/auditory),puzzles and environment monitoring activities which are used to assess the symptoms


Technologies Used

Android Studio-Java,React-Redux,Node-express and Unity(C#),Firebase
